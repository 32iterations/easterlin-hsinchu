## Executive Summary

This comprehensive research report analyzes movable partition systems, modular furniture, and adaptive space solutions for multi-generational facilities, specifically targeting the 赤土崎全齡社福樞紐 project. The research covers product specifications, cost analysis, case studies, technical requirements, and implementation strategies for creating flexible spaces that serve elderly care, childcare, and youth programs within a single facility.

**Key Findings:**
- Operable partition walls range from $15-150 per square foot depending on operation type and acoustic requirements
- Leading manufacturers (Modernfold, Hufcor, Screenflex) offer systems with STC ratings from 40-56
- Flexible space design can reduce renovation costs by 25-50% and construction time by up to 50%
- Intergenerational facilities require specialized design with flexible, transformable spaces
- Fire-rated and ADA-compliant options are available for healthcare applications
- Expected lifespan of quality movable walls: 30+ years with proper maintenance

### 3.4 Key Insights from Case Studies

#### **Design Principles:**
1. **Household Model:** Smaller units (15-20 people) create home-like environments
2. **Flexible Multipurpose Spaces:** Large rooms dividable for multiple daily functions
3. **Intergenerational Zones:** Dedicated spaces facilitating cross-age interaction
4. **Universal Grid Design:** Future-proofing for technology and program changes
5. **Regulatory Compliance:** Early coordination with licensing agencies

#### **Financial Benefits:**
- **Renovation Cost Reduction:** 25-50% savings vs. fixed walls
- **Construction Time Savings:** Up to 50% reduction with modular methods
- **Operational Efficiency:** Reduced vacancy through flexible space allocation
- **Energy Savings:** Defined HVAC zones, close off unused sections

#### **Operational Outcomes:**
- **Resident Satisfaction:** 14 of 17 measures improved (Menno Haven)
- **Infection Control:** Better containment with flexible isolation spaces
- **Program Capacity:** Multiple programs in same space throughout day
- **Staff Efficiency:** Easier care delivery in household models

### 8.2 Design Best Practices

#### **Acoustic Design**
- **Target STC 50+** for spaces requiring speech privacy (elderly counseling, youth tutoring during intergenerational activities)
- **Background Noise:** Maintain ≤35 dBA in learning/care spaces (ANSI standard)
- **Material Selection:** Premium acoustic panels for high-use partitions

#### **Flexibility Principles**
- **Universal Grid Design:** Plan electrical, HVAC, lighting on grid for future reconfigurations
- **Modular Furniture:** All furniture should be movable, stackable, or reconfigurable
- **Technology Infrastructure:** Pre-wire for future smart controls, audiovisual systems

#### **Safety & Accessibility**
- **Egress:** Ensure partition configurations never block emergency exits
- **ADA Pass Doors:** Minimum one accessible pass door per partition
- **Operating Force:** ≤5 lbs for all manual operations
- **Visual Clarity:** Clear door perimeters for emergency identification

#### **Operational Efficiency**
- **Motorized Systems:** For partitions >100 m² to reduce staff physical demands
- **Pre-Programmed Layouts:** Store 5-10 common configurations for one-button transformation
- **Storage:** Dedicated space for portable partitions when not in use
- **Maintenance Access:** Easy access to tracks and mechanical systems

### 8.3 Sustainability Considerations

#### **Material Selection**
- **Recycled Content:** Specify partitions with recycled materials (Modernfold sustainability line)
- **LEED Contribution:** Document for potential LEED credits
- **Lifespan:** Prioritize 30+ year systems to reduce replacement waste

#### **Energy Efficiency**
- **HVAC Zoning:** Design partitions to create defined zones for heating/cooling optimization
- **Daylighting:** Specify glass partition options where appropriate for natural light
- **Lifecycle Cost:** Calculate 30-year operational savings vs. initial investment

#### **Community Input**
- **Timing:** Months 1-3 (design phase)
- **Methods:**
  - Public workshops showcasing flexible space concepts
  - Mockup demonstrations of partition systems
  - Intergenerational programming previews
- **Objective:** Build community support and gather operational insights

#### **Operator Involvement**
